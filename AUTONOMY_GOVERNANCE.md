# Bifrost Autonomy Governance System
## Controlled Autonomous Execution Framework for Asgard Aerospace

Version: 1.0  
System: Bifrost OS  
Organization: Asgard Aerospace

---

# 1. Purpose

The Autonomy Governance System defines how autonomous systems operate inside Bifrost.

This document establishes:
- operational autonomy boundaries
- approval requirements
- execution authority
- risk controls
- audit requirements
- escalation behavior
- human-in-the-loop doctrine
- trust architecture

Bifrost is NOT an uncontrolled AI system.

It is:
- a governed operational intelligence environment
- a controlled execution system
- a human-directed aerospace operating system

Autonomy exists to:
- accelerate execution
- compress complexity
- increase operational awareness
- reduce repetitive work
- amplify strategic cognition

Autonomy must NEVER:
- bypass governance
- operate without accountability
- mutate critical systems silently
- communicate externally without approval

---

# 2. Core Philosophy

## Human Command Authority Is Absolute

Humans remain the final authority for:
- strategic direction
- external communication
- mission escalation
- operational commitments
- supplier relationships
- investor interactions

Autonomy supports operators.

Autonomy does NOT replace operators.

---

## Controlled Autonomy

Bifrost autonomy must feel:
- bounded
- explainable
- observable
- tactical
- reliable
- aerospace-grade

NOT:
- magical
- uncontrolled
- unpredictable
- opaque

---

## Explainability First

Every autonomous action must answer:

1. Why was this proposed?
2. What data informed it?
3. What mission is affected?
4. What happens if accepted?
5. What happens if ignored?

---

# 3. Autonomy Classification System

Autonomous behaviors are divided into operational classes.

---

# 3.1 Passive Intelligence

## Definition

Read-only operational cognition.

---

## Examples

- summarization
- relevance scoring
- intelligence tagging
- risk detection
- relationship analysis
- mission pressure analysis
- recommendation generation

---

## Governance Level

Low risk.

No approval required.

---

# 3.2 Assisted Execution

## Definition

Autonomy prepares actions for operator review.

---

## Examples

- draft investor outreach
- prepare executive briefing
- generate supplier recommendations
- create task suggestions
- generate opportunity summaries

---

## Governance Level

Medium risk.

Operator review required before execution.

---

# 3.3 Controlled Operational Actions

## Definition

Autonomy may perform bounded internal operations.

---

## Examples

- create internal tasks
- reprioritize queues
- update internal scores
- route intelligence signals
- generate mission alerts
- classify records

---

## Governance Level

Medium risk.

Requires:
- audit logging
- traceability
- rollback capability

---

# 3.4 Approval-Gated External Actions

## Definition

Autonomy proposes actions affecting external systems or relationships.

---

## Examples

- investor outreach
- supplier communication
- executive messaging
- external data mutation
- partner recommendations
- mission escalation

---

## Governance Level

High risk.

Explicit approval required.

---

# 3.5 Restricted Actions

## Definition

Actions autonomy may NEVER perform independently.

---

## Examples

- financial commitments
- contract execution
- supplier termination
- investor negotiation
- strategic pivots
- destructive deletion
- security permission changes

---

## Governance Level

Prohibited without direct operator execution.

---

Material operational authority remains human-controlled.

---

## Human Responsibilities

## Autonomy Responsibilities

Autonomy:

# 5. Approval System

## Approval Philosophy

Approvals protect:
- operational integrity
- strategic relationships
- mission stability
- external trust
- governance compliance

---

## Approval Requirements

Approval is required for:
- outbound communication
- supplier assignments
- investor messaging
- external integrations
- mission escalation
- operational commitments

---

## Approval Workflow

```text
Proposed Action
    ↓
Risk Classification
    ↓
Approval Queue
    ↓
Operator Review
    ↓
Approved / Rejected / Modified
    ↓
Execution
    ↓
Audit Log
```

Logs must include:
- action type
- initiating system
- reasoning
- confidence score
- operator approvals
- affected entities
- mission linkage
- execution outcome
- timestamps

## Audit Persistence

Autonomy history must remain:
- immutable
- queryable
- historically accessible

# 8. Operational Boundaries

## Autonomy Must Remain Bounded

The autonomy layer must:
- operate within defined scopes
- avoid uncontrolled propagation
- preserve operational predictability

## Boundary Examples

- recommend suppliers

- prioritize opportunities

- send investor emails automatically
- alter mission state without review
- commit external agreements
- change governance permissions
