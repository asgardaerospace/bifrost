# 3. Mission Architecture

## Mission Layers

Missions exist across three strategic layers.

---

## Layer 1 — Strategic Missions

High-level company objectives.

### Examples
- Raise Series A
- Build defense manufacturing capability
- Establish European operations

### Characteristics
- executive-owned
- long duration
- cross-system impact
- high strategic value

---

## Layer 2 — Operational Missions

Execution-oriented initiatives supporting strategic missions.

### Examples
- onboard five composite suppliers
- secure investor introductions
- complete ORION manufacturing milestone

### Characteristics
- measurable
- execution-focused
- cross-functional
- time-bound

---

## Layer 3 — Tactical Missions

Short-term focused execution tasks.

### Examples
- prepare investor memo
- schedule supplier audit
- finalize customer proposal

### Characteristics
- rapid execution
- lower scope
- highly actionable

---

# 4. Mission Lifecycle

## Lifecycle States

Every mission progresses through defined operational states.

---

## Proposed

Mission exists conceptually but is not active.

### Characteristics
- under evaluation
- incomplete scope
- limited operational visibility

---

## Active

Mission is actively executing.

### Characteristics
- visible in executive layer
- contributes to operational pressure
- receives intelligence propagation
- affects prioritization

---

## Escalated

Mission requires elevated attention.

### Causes
- blocked execution
- supplier failure
- funding pressure
- timeline compression
- strategic risk increase

### Effects
- increased visibility
- queue prioritization
- leadership escalation

---

## Blocked

Mission cannot progress.

### Causes
- dependency failure
- missing approvals
- supplier gaps
- funding gaps
- external risk

### Effects
- critical operational pressure
- top-tier visibility
- intervention recommendations

---

## Completed

Mission objectives achieved.

### Effects
- historical archival
- graph persistence
- execution analytics retained

---

## Archived

Mission no longer strategically relevant.

### Effects
- removed from active prioritization
- retained historically
